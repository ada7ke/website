My Teaching Philosophy

When I teach, I focus on building real understanding of concepts and strong problem-solving skills, not just rote memorization. Every programming language has its own syntax, but the core ideas behind them stay the same. Once you understand those foundations, you can adapt to any language out there. The same is true for problem solving, as real-world challenges are always new, so memorizing solutions isn’t enough. What actually matters is learning how to think through problems and approach them effectively.
